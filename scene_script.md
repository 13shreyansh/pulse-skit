# Pulse Skit — Scene-by-Scene Script (Phase 1)

## Overview

Total scenes: 14
Estimated skit duration at natural narration pace: 40–50 seconds
Tone: Comedy (Scenes 1–12) → Abrupt silence → Serious (Scene 13) → Transition to product (Scene 14)

---

## Scene 1: Establishing Shot — Marina Bay

**Image description:** Wide cinematic shot of Singapore's Marina Bay waterfront at golden hour. Calm water, modern skyline in background, pedestrian/cycling path visible in foreground. Beautiful, peaceful, nothing wrong yet.

**Text overlay:** None

**Animation:** Slow zoom in (Ken Burns), 3 seconds

**Transition to next:** Crossfade

---

## Scene 2: The Cyclist

**Image description:** A young man (20s, casual t-shirt, shorts) cycling along a waterfront path. Side-angle view. He looks relaxed, maybe wearing earbuds. The path is smooth, the day is perfect.

**Text overlay:** None

**Animation:** Slow pan left-to-right (following motion direction)

**Transition to next:** Quick cut (no fade — sudden)

---

## Scene 3: The Fall

**Image description:** The same cyclist mid-fall — bike tipping sideways, the rider's body hitting the pavement. Dramatic angle (slightly low, looking up). His leg is bent awkwardly. The bicycle is tangled. Motion blur on the falling body.

**Text overlay:** None

**Animation:** Quick shake effect on scene entry (simulating impact), then still with slow zoom

**Transition to next:** Crossfade (0.5s)

---

## Scene 4: Bystander 1 — The Google Doctor

**Image description:** A person (20s, standing) looking down at their phone with a deeply confused and slightly panicked expression. In the background (blurred), someone is lying on the ground. The phone screen is glowing but we can't see what's on it.

**Text overlay:** None (this scene sets up Scene 4b)

**Animation:** Character fades in from slight blur to sharp. Phone screen glows.

**Transition to next:** Zoom into the phone (transition into 4b)

---

## Scene 4b: The Phone Screen — "47 Steps"

**Image description:** A close-up of a phone screen showing a fake article. Header: "How to Help Someone With a Broken Leg: A Complete 47-Step Guide." Visible steps: Step 1: Remain calm. Step 2: Assess the situation. Step 3: Determine if the fracture is open or closed. Step 4: Recall your first aid training (if applicable). Step 5: Check for signs of shock...

**Note:** This is a designed static asset (not AI-generated). Clean, realistic phone-screen mockup with article text.

**Text overlay:** None (the article text IS the comedy)

**Animation:** Slow scroll up (simulating reading), slight zoom in

**Transition to next:** Hard cut

---

## Scene 5: Bystander 2 — The Overconfident Helper

**Image description:** A person (20s-30s, athletic build, rolled-up sleeves) kneeling next to the injured person on the ground. They're confidently reaching for the injured leg with both hands. Their facial expression is pure confidence — "I've got this." The injured person's face shows terror.

**Text overlay:** "Trust the process bro" (appears after 1 second, bottom-center, white text with slight shadow)

**Animation:** Character slides in from right. Text fades in after delay.

**Transition to next:** Hard cut

---

## Scene 6: Bystander 3 — The Content Creator

**Image description:** A person (20s) holding their phone vertically at arm's length, clearly filming. They're slightly adjusting their angle for better framing. Maybe one hand is fixing their hair. In the blurred background, the injured person is on the ground. The filmmaker's expression is focused — like they're composing a shot, not helping.

**Text overlay:** "Wait, do that again" (appears after 1 second, bottom-center)

**Animation:** Subtle tilt animation (phone adjusting angle). Maybe a brief flash effect (like a camera flash).

**Transition to next:** Hard cut

---

## Scene 7: Bystander 4 — The Panicker

**Image description:** A person (20s) with both hands on their head, mouth wide open, eyes wide. Pure frozen panic. They're standing upright, not kneeling — completely useless. Background shows the scene of the accident.

**Text overlay:** "MA, SOMEONE IS DYING" (appears after 0.5 seconds, large, slightly shaky text)

**Animation:** Character vibrates/shakes slightly (panic tremor effect)

**Transition to next:** Crossfade (1s, slower — transitioning to the hospital sequence)

---

## Scene 8: Carrying the Injured

**Image description:** Two people carrying/supporting an injured person between them, moving urgently down a city street. The injured person's leg is dangling. Everyone looks exhausted and panicked. The street is urban Singapore — shophouses or HDB blocks in background. Motion and urgency in the composition.

**Text overlay:** None

**Animation:** Pan left-to-right (suggesting forward movement), slightly faster than other scenes

**Transition to next:** Hard cut

---

## Scene 9: Hospital 1 — Rejection

**Image description:** The group arriving at a hospital entrance (glass doors, clinical lighting, "Emergency" sign visible). A person at a reception desk is visible through the glass, hand raised in a "stop" or "no" gesture. The group outside looks desperate.

**Text overlay:** "Full." (appears in red, large, center-screen, after 0.5s)

**Animation:** Zoom toward the hospital entrance, then the red text slams in

**Transition to next:** Hard cut (fast)

---

## Scene 10: Hospital 2 — Rejection

**Image description:** A different hospital exterior (different architecture, maybe more run-down). The same group, visibly more exhausted — sweat, slouching, the injured person looking worse. A staff member at the entrance is shaking their head.

**Text overlay:** "No trauma center." (appears in red, same style as Scene 9)

**Animation:** Same pattern — zoom in, text slams

**Transition to next:** Hard cut (fast)

---

## Scene 11: Hospital 3 — Closed

**Image description:** A hospital or clinic door. It's closed. A sign is taped to the door. The group is standing in front of it, completely defeated. Someone might be on their knees. The sign reads "Back at 3:00 PM — Lunch."

**Note:** The sign is a designed static overlay (not baked into the AI image) so it's clearly readable.

**Text overlay:** The sign itself serves as the punchline. No additional overlay needed.

**Animation:** Slow zoom into the door/sign. Desaturation begins (color draining from the scene).

**Transition to next:** Slow fade to black (1.5s — this is the TONE SHIFT)

---

## Scene 12: Defeat

**Image description:** The group slumped against a wall outside. Sitting on the ground. The injured person lying flat. Everyone's heads are down. Completely hopeless. Muted colors, harsh overhead lighting (like a street at night or under fluorescent hospital corridor lights).

**Text overlay:** None

**Animation:** Slow zoom out (pulling away from them, creating distance/isolation). Full desaturation to near-grayscale.

**Transition to next:** Fade to pure black (2s)

---

## Scene 13: The Turn — Statistics

**Image description:** No image. Pure black background. White text appears one statement at a time.

**Text sequence (each appears one at a time, centered):**

1. "That was a skit."
2. "But across Southeast Asia — that's reality."
3. "In Jakarta, 75% of emergency ambulance responses take over 30 minutes." (Source: BMC Health Services Research, 2022)
4. "Only 9% of trauma patients in Indonesia reach hospital by ambulance. Most are carried on motorbikes." (Source: Brice et al., 2022)
5. "Over 70% of the world's population cannot access safe, timely emergency surgical care." (Source: WHO / Critical Care, 2024)
6. "The first hospital you reach isn't always the right one. And when it's not — that's a life."

**Animation:** Each text line fades in one at a time (1.5s per line). Previous line fades to 50% opacity when next appears.

**Transition to next:** Fade to black, then fade in

---

## Scene 14: The Transition to Product

**Image description:** No image. Clean dark background (very dark navy or black) with centered text.

**Text:** "What if one tap could replace all of that?"

**Animation:** Text fades in slowly (2s). Holds for 2 seconds. Then fades out.

**Note:** After this, you cut to your Pulse platform screen recording. This scene is the bridge.
